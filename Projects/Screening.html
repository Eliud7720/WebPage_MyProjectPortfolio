Virtual screening of the COCONUT and ReFRAME databases using machine learning models - Thesis project 6

About this project

Apply the selected best model for each protein to the database to predict every compound‚Äôs activity for each target. For each compound‚Äìprotein pair compute the probability of belonging to class 1 (active), then compare those probabilities to rank candidates (for example, a compound with 0.9 is preferable to one with 0.7). Finally, filter and prioritize compounds that show high probabilities across multiple proteins to identify the most promising multi-target candidates.

Objective

Carry out predictions using the best ML models for each protein and identify the top candidate compounds based on those predictions.

Development

Data collection

The databases COCONUT and ReframeDB were downloaded in CSV format, containing nearly 700,000 compounds in total for prediction. These files were loaded into Python as pandas DataFrames and then merged into a single table named ‚Äúcombined bases‚Äù, which includes only three columns: Identifier, SMILES, and Compound Name.

Data Processing

After merging the databases, the Python library RDKit was used to remove salts and ions from all compounds. Additionally, compounds already present in the datasets were excluded. Compounds with missing SMILES were also removed, while those with missing names were retained since most compounds do not have a name until they are patented. Finally, compounds with duplicate identifiers or duplicate SMILES were eliminated.

Predictions

Once the data was processed, the ECFP4 molecular fingerprint was calculated for all compounds, since it is the one used in the best models for each protein. With this information, the probability of belonging to class 1 (active) was predicted for every compound across the 4 proteins, resulting in a total of 2,800,000 predictions

Conclusions

  • Nearly 700,000 compounds from the COCONUT and ReFrameDB databases were merged and processed, removing salts, ions, duplicates, and compounds with missing SMILES.
  • ECFP4 fingerprints were calculated and used to predict the probability of activity (class 1) across 4 target proteins, resulting in 2,800,000 predictions.
  • A large number of compounds show high probability (‚â•0.9) of inhibiting EGFR and MAPK1, while far fewer compounds are likely to inhibit SRC and STAT3, indicating that multi-target candidates are mostly EGFR/MAPK1-focused.
  • After identifying shared compounds and applying Lipinski‚Äôs rules to ensure oral bioavailability, 594 promising multi-target compounds were selected.
  • Further analysis including molecular docking, interaction analysis, toxicity, and cost assessment is ongoing, ultimately narrowing down to 4 highly promising compounds.
